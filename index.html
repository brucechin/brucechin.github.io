About Me

I graduated from Shanghai Jiao Tong University with a B.Eng. in Computer Software Engineering in 2019 summer. I spent one year at Northwestern CS and then transferred to UC Santa Barbara to continue my Ph.D. journey in data system and applied cryptography. I currently work closely with Prof. Shumo Chu.

My main research interest lies in applying modern cryptography techniques like secure computation, zero-knowledge proof and blockchain as well as trusted hardware like Intel SGX into data processing and storage system in untrusted environment to obtain more security and privacy guarantee. Although modern cryptography has made amazing progress over the past decades, few of these theoretical advances have been incorporated into practical systems. I believe there are opportunities via a co-design of systems and cryptography.
